A Million Walks in the Park
January 4, 2018

Parks Project Finally Out!

Our project using cell phone app data to understand human mobility and public space land use in New York City parks is just out. We analyze NYC parks on different scales and different levels of specificity to gain a beter understanding of how people use public parks in the city and which places in the park are most popular.

Spatial Data Science Conference
December 1, 2017

CARTO's First Spatial Data Science Conference

I am pleased to announce that the Spatial Data Science Community (i.e. the research team at CARTO and the University of Chicago's Center for Spatial Data Science) has organized our first conference. The conference tooks place on December 1 in our office in Bushwick and we were positively surprised to find the amount of interest in this topic!

The goal of this conference was to bring together practioners of spatial data science across different fields in industries and academia, who all have to address the common problem of Tobler's first law.

Conference presentations will be out on the website shortly.

NACIS
Nov 10, 2017

Ghost Cities Presentation at NACIS

I gave a talk at this year's NACIS conference about work I did modeling ghost cities in China using amenities data while I was at the Civic Data Design Lab. This project uses a standard suite of amenities to find residential regions that are far away from this suite and are, therefore, good "ghost city" candidates.

Bloomberg D4GX
September 24, 2017

Presentation at Bloomberg's D4GX on Parks Analysis

I presented at this year's Data for Social Good Exchange on our project analyzing mobility patterns in New York City's Parks. The project uses a hierarchical clustering model to find hotspots of various scales for all Manhattan and Brooklyn parks, and then finds which appear to be more conducive to social interaction.
